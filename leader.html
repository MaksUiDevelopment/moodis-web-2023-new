Our Leaders

Golda Meir
Golda Meir served as Prime Minister from 1969 to 1974. During her tenure as Prime Minister, she decisively led the Yom Kippur War. Golda Meir was a prominent and brave leader in the history of the State of Israel.
Benjamin Netanyahu
Benjamin Netanyahu is the Prime Minister of Israel, the leader of the Likud Party, and a member of the Knesset on its behalf. Netanyahu is the first Prime Minister of Israel born after the establishment of the State of Israel. From 1984 to 1988, he served as Israel's Ambassador to the UN. He later joined the Likud Party, became a member of the 12th Knesset, and served as Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs. In 1993, he was elected as the Chairman of Likud.
Theodor Herzl
Benjamin Ze'ev Theodor Herzl was a Jewish journalist, lawyer, writer, playwright, and statesman. He developed the idea of political Zionism and founded the first Zionist Congress.
David Ben-Gurion
David Ben-Gurion was a figure of the Second Aliyah, a leader in the labor movement, a member of the Poale Zion and "Ahdut HaAvoda" parties, the first General Secretary of the Histadrut labor federation in Israel, and the first leader of Mapai. As the Chairman of the Jewish Agency Executive and the Chairman of the Zionist Council, he led the Jewish settlement in the Land of Israel during the British Mandate.
